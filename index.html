Executive Summary

We are not inventing a brand. We are recovering one.

The Tropics is one of the most photographed and storied small hotels in the Coachella Valley: a 92 key Polynesian themed resort at the gateway to South Palm Canyon Drive, with documented ties to Oceanic Arts, the founding of Tiki Oasis, and a celebrity history reaching back to the Rat Pack era. Today it operates as a tired motor lodge running well below its potential and significantly undercapitalized in both design and operations. That gap is the opportunity.

A disciplined $5.5M design and repositioning program, focused first on the parts of the guest experience that move rate, transforms a motor lodge into a curated, immersive Midcentury Polynesian resort with the largest pool footprint in the desert market and an outdoor program no boutique competitor can match.

Palm Springs is the right market and now is the right moment. The city sits 90 miles from Los Angeles and has trained a generation of Angelenos to treat the desert as their long weekend reset. Midcentury modernism is the city's global brand, Modernism Week is a built in press and audience engine, and the experiential, design forward end of the market is where rate and PR concentrate. In a supply heavy market, the newest, best designed product wins.

The creative strategy pairs a hospitality grade interior and concept lead with a local Palm Springs architect of record, a tiki heritage specialist for the soul of the property, an awards grade landscape vision for the pool and lawn, and a wellness and technology layer that quietly raises the experience. Tiki heritage is treated with sophistication, not kitsch. The awards roadmap is sequenced honestly against the renovation timeline, with near term local and press wins now and the major hospitality design awards targeted for the cycles that follow completion.

Awards Roadmap

From local recognition now to the major design awards after completion.

Most major design awards require a completed project, and several use a defined completion window. Because the renovation completes after the heavy work window, the major hospitality design push falls in the cycles that follow completion. The near term plays are local recognition, Modernism Week visibility, and concept press, which also warm up jurors and editors for later.

Sequencing. Open the local and Modernism Week relationships now, build the press story through concept and construction, and stage the national and global submissions for the first full eligibility cycle after completion. Enter the same project in multiple categories where rules allow, since the bar, the guestrooms, the public space, the landscape, and the overall renovation are each strong on their own.
